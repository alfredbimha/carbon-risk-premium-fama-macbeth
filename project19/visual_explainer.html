Understanding the Carbon Risk Premium

Replicating Bolton & Kacperczyk (2021): Do investors demand extra return for holding high-carbon stocks?

The Carbon Premium Hypothesis

If carbon emissions represent a risk (regulatory, reputational, transitional), investors should demand higher returns for holding high-emission stocks — a "carbon risk premium."

Monthly Return = γ₀ + γ₁ × log(Carbon Intensity) + γ₂ × log(Size) + γ₃ × BM + ε

If γ₁ > 0 and significant: investors ARE pricing carbon risk.

Fama-MacBeth Regressions

The Fama-MacBeth procedure is a two-step method that handles cross-sectional correlation:

1Each month: run cross-sectional regression
2Average the monthly coefficients
3Test if average ≠ 0

This gives us 80+ monthly estimates of the carbon coefficient — we can see how it varies over time and test its statistical significance.
