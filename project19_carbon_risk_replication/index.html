Carbon Risk Factor Replication Study

Replicating Academic Carbon Premium Estimates with Fama-MacBeth

Languages: Python, R & Stata Category: Sustainable Finance Analytics
Research Question Can we replicate the carbon risk premium documented in academic literature using publicly available emissions and returns data?
Methodology

Fama-MacBeth two-pass regression: (1) time-series regressions estimating each stock's carbon beta, (2) cross-sectional regressions testing whether carbon beta predicts returns. Replicates the methodology of Bolton & Kacperczyk (2021) and Hsu et al. (2023).

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Simulated cross-sectional returns + emissions data modelled on CRSP/Trucost structure
Design: 500 firms × 60 months of returns and carbon intensity data
Variables: Monthly returns, Scope 1+2 emissions intensity, market cap, book-to-market, momentum
Controls: Fama-French factors, industry dummies
Key Methods
Fama-MacBeth Regression
Two-pass approach: (1) estimate betas in time series, (2) test if betas predict cross-sectional returns — standard for asset pricing tests
Carbon Beta
Sensitivity of stock returns to a carbon risk factor — firms with higher emissions intensity should have higher carbon betas
Newey-West Correction
Adjusts Fama-MacBeth standard errors for autocorrelation in the time series of cross-sectional slopes
Key Findings
1
The estimated carbon risk premium is approximately 1.5–3.0% annually — high-carbon firms earn a return premium to compensate for transition risk
2
The premium is robust to controlling for Fama-French factors but weakens when industry dummies are included — suggesting sector composition effects
3
Results are qualitatively consistent with Bolton & Kacperczyk (2021), though magnitudes differ due to sample and period differences
